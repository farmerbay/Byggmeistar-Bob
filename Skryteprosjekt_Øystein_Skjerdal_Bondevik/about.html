Bob the Builder

Bob the Builder is an animated TV-show created by Keith Chapman. The show was produced by Hot-animation until 2012, but today Mattel is in charge of the production. Bob the Builder are well known in many countries, although it was manly produced for the people living on the British Islands. Bob the Builder is a show meaning to teach children about the importance of helping other with their problems, no matter how big it is, and each episode is about one of Bob’s projects. The show has changed name a number of times over the years. First it was named “Bob the Builder”, but after the producers decided to name the series. “Project: Build It” went on from 2005 to 2009, and “Ready Steady, Build!” was the name until 2012. Bob the Builder has become very famous for its catchy theme-song; “Can We Fix It?”, a song written by Paul K. Joyce. This song went on to become a million-selling number one hit in the UK. In all the show has a total of 355 episodes divided into 20 seasons of different series.

The idea behind

The idea behind building this page, was to make a page which both pleases the old fans of Bob the Builder, but also make a place for new Bob the Builders fans, or even the people which are not yet a fan. I searched the internet for information about Bob, but this was not easy to find, and especially any information in Norwegian. This I could not be pleased with and decided to build the page. It is also a school-project, which might be the real reason.
